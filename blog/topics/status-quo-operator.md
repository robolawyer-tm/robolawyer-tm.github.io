Some conflicts aren't about what's true. The truth is already there — already inside the system that holds the outcome in place. The status quo operator describes what happens next.

---

**The pattern.** When a prosecutor supports a defendant's innocence and the defendant remains imprisoned, the institution is not deceiving anyone. It knows what happened. The resistance is structural: reversing the prior conviction carries costs the structure cannot absorb — admission of error, erosion of authority, liability for the original decision. The conviction became load-bearing for the institution's integrity, independent of its accuracy.

**Why it's distinct.** Most conflict analysis targets the gap between what happened and what was recorded — deception, omission, reframing. The status quo operator targets the gap between what the institution already knows and what it does. More evidence doesn't resolve it. Truth-telling doesn't resolve it. The blocking mechanism operates below the level where those interventions reach.

**Why DNA exoneration data is valuable.** When prosecutors actively support innocence and imprisonment continues, the "we didn't know" defense is gone. What remains is pure structural inertia — documented in public court records, with parties, positions, and outcomes all on record. Baseline established by physical evidence, not testimony. The cleanest signal for this specific operator.

**The modeling target.** Most conflict tools try to establish truth. This project models the conditions under which a structure can release a prior commitment without collapsing its own authority. That is a navigational problem, not an evidentiary one. No existing conflict framework addresses this specifically.

**Connection to the operator framework.** The status quo operator is one of a set of divergence operators the pipeline will formalize — omission, reframing, inversion, temporal displacement, category collapse, status quo. Each describes how a gap is maintained between what is known and what is done. The status quo operator is the most dangerous because it is immune to truth.

**The `operators: []` slot.** In `logos_social_v01.json`, every social layer carries an `operators: []` array. At the `society` layer (size 1500, gesellschaft, governance rule-based), the status quo operator is fully operational — archive transmission, sovereign authority, cooperative violated. The slot waits for the inference store to validate this before it's filled.
